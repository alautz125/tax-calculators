2026 Tax Proposals — Three-Way Comparison

Compare net federal income tax liability under current law, the Van Hollen proposal, and the Booker proposal. Van Hollen introduces an Alternative Maximum Tax (AMaT) — taxpayers pay the lower of regular income tax or 25.5% of income above a floor, with the benefit phasing out above income ceilings — plus a high-income surcharge of 5–12% on MAGI above $1M (Van Hollen only, not current law). Booker expands the standard deduction and provides a fully refundable per-child CTC.
